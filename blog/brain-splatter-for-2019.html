Brain-Splatter for 2019

Sat 05 January 2019
Tags: [Meta]

This is going to be a brain-splatter for myself at the beginning of 2019. The idea is that I will come back and read this and get inspiration from my older self. There is a narrative that says that you should not let the kid in you die. I try my best to do that, but what if shit hits the fan. I want to have a backup so that I can look back for reference. This is for the future me.

Also, this is my blog. I can do whatever I want with it.

Write more. It's Okay!

So I realize that I write less in my blog. That should change. The blog is meant more for me and less for the reader. There are better blogs out there for that purpose. I write because I want to read it later.

Complexity

Those who have seen me know that I for some reason find a lot of things interesting. It's not rare for my friends to see me fully geek out on toilets, or a nice jacket or a cut on the chin or a broken leg or a hole in the ground or anything for that matter.

I love complexity; I find complexity marvellous and breadthtaking. I love to think about complex interconnected systems. in fact most things in life are massivly complex if you think deeply enough.

I read Carl Sagan's Cosmos during my school days and this book literally changed my life. I realized after reading this book that the world is much more beautiful than it looks. :-)

Also, the world is much more complex that it looks. That is what makes it so beautiful in the first place.

I am also very very hyperactive. Its quite difficult for me to stop and do absolutely nothing. If you see me sitting somewhere, I am probably building a spaceship in my head.

Dabbling with Insignificance.

Space has always fascinated me. Ever since I started to comprehend the scale at which the universe operates I can not help but think about the insignificance of our short lives. We are truely insignificant at a cosmic scale. I remember reading about the cosmic calendar. Carl Sagan said that if we laid out the entire history of the universe in the form of a calendar such that the big bang would be Januray 1st 00:00:00 and present day would be 31st December 11:59:59 then all of human existence would be only the last 13 seconds. Yeah. Everything that we have till date is just the last 13 seconds on a cosmic scale.

But Carl says "we are made of star-stuff; we are a way of the cosmos to know itself"

Isn't that powerful? :-)

Think deeply.

Think deeply. I have observed that often the most banal things in life reveal their true beauty if you care enough to think deeply. What does thinking deeply mean ?

Take any simple regular object. A pen. Simple enough right ? Not really. Hold on for a minute and think about all the coplexities involved into making that pen. The probably has a refill. That refill probably has some ink in it and a nib. That nib probably has a metal ball on top or some other technology. How did all of that come into existence ? How did humans even think of this ?

It took us 13 seconds to start from scratch to building bricks that can do magic. We call them computers. It took us 13 seconds to start form scratch and to go to the moon. It took us 13 seconds to start from scratch and build everything that we have today.

Be curious

Unlike what some people I met think, there is no upper limit to how much a human being can store in his/her head. Or even if there is, there is no scientific literature to support that claim.

So, be curious. Don't stop asking "why" untill you are satisfied. And do not be satisfied easily. Be curous about the complexity. Learn about it. Marvel at it.

Stop complaining.

A lot of times when I wanted to do something, I would just procrastinate on not doing it and instead figure out multiple ways to not do it. Most of the time I would end up complaining about how I could not do something and wait for someone else to rescue me.

This is not a good thing. I've wasted enough time doing this. Let's do this less from now on.

Very recently I have started taking the other approach. Do everything in my power and then complain. Because lets face it, there will always be something that will not be in my power so instead of focusing in that why not focus on the part that is within my power and do that. Once that is done then we can worry about the part that is not within my power.

Be skeptical

I have trust issues. I tend to not trust information unless its coming from a very credible source and it has a lot of consensus.

This is kind of good as it means that I am bullet proof to rumurs and other things, but the down side is that I tend to ignore good advise just because it is coming from someone who is not me and that advise is based on personal experience which can be very very subjective.

But being skeptical is good. Keep being skeptical. Don't believe things easily.

Be flexible.

Don't be rigid in your beliefs. Base them on facts. If the facts change, update your beliefs.

Adopt the Baesian mothodology of thinging and reasoning about the world.

Dont be scared of conflicting information

Conflicting information means that there is something deeping going on. Dig deeper. Figure it out.
