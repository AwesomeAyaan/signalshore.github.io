Can you define a process and a procedure ?

Think about it for a minute. Both of these terms are very common terms in Computer Science yet its so difficult to define them.

In this post I will break down what these two seemingly synonymous words mean and we will also have some nice Scheme programming tricks.

Procedure

A procedure is something that defines the goal.

In computer science terms; we are defining a procedure when we are writing a function. The function most of the timie tell the computer what to do and the how to do it is often determined by the internals of the computer (this is not a completely correct statement; but that is not what we are talking about right not so this is a good enough description).

Process

A process is the act of doing something (maybe this doing is based on a procedure defined previously).

In Computer Science terms; a process is something that the System (system is an abstraction that can mean aything in this context viz. Operating System, Porgramming System etc) uses to execute a procedure. For example, we might have written a simple one-line procedure but in order to perform that action the System may have needed to conjure up more than one processes.
